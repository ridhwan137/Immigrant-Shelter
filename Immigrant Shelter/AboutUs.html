ABOUT US

Many migrant workers in Malaysia were already living in unsanitary conditions prior to the COVID-19 outbreak. These has caused many migrant workers to move out as they were prone to viruses and infections. Many companies had no businesses and were shut down during the pandemic out of bankruptcy. So, Immigrant Shelter is tasked for all actions connected to assisting immigrants, including temporary shelter, registration, paperwork, and immigration status determination, as well as the provision of shelter and basic necessities.

Objectives

To provide an excellent home care service to migrant workers.

To ease migrant workers in finding a temporary place to stay.

To help minimise the burden and living cost of migrant workers during the pandemic.

To reduce the probability of migrant workers contracting to the virus.

Project Significance

This project is developed mainly for the migrant workers with dreadful living condition or without a place to live in.

To ease the process of migrant workers finding for home care services provided by the locals.

To help the home care service providers to enlist their house or locations and help the needful people.

To assist in reducing cost of rental for migrant workers who has no place to live in.

Problem Statement

Many migrant workers in Malaysia were already living in overcrowded and unsanitary conditions prior to the COVID-19 outbreak. It was not uncommon for them to be housed in converted shipping containers with limited access to clean water and sanitary amenities, for instance, in the construction sectors. These has caused many migrant workers to move out as they were prone to viruses and infections. There was no option for the affected migrant workers to seek help from during the outbreak. Many companies had no businesses and were shut down during the pandemic out of bankruptcy. This has given rise to income problem to workers especially to those migrant workers that suffered without a comfortable and clean place to live.
